function doPost(e) {
  try {
    var sheet = SpreadsheetApp.getActiveSpreadsheet().getActiveSheet();
    var data = JSON.parse(e.postData.contents);
    var timestamp = new Date().toLocaleString('en-IN');
    sheet.appendRow([
      timestamp,
      data.date,
      data.totalCal,
      data.burned,
      data.steps,
      data.water,
      data.weight || '',
      data.meals
    ]);
    return ContentService.createTextOutput(JSON.stringify({status:'ok'}))
      .setMimeType(ContentService.MimeType.JSON);
  } catch(err) {
    return ContentService.createTextOutput(JSON.stringify({status:'error',msg:err.toString()}))
      .setMimeType(ContentService.MimeType.JSON);
  }
}

📚 Nutrition Guide

Vitamins · Probiotics · Weight Loss Tips

☀️
Vitamin A
Eyes · Skin · Immunity
▼
Essential for vision (especially night vision), skin health, and immune function. Deficiency causes dry eyes and skin.
🥗 Best Indian Food Sources
Sweet potatoCarrotPumpkin Spinach (palak)Drumstick leaves (moringa)Amaranth (rajgira) MangoPapayaEgg yolk Whole milkGhee (small amount)
💡 Eat with a little fat (ghee/oil) — Vitamin A is fat-soluble and absorbs better that way.
🩸
Vitamin B12
Energy · Brain · Red blood cells
▼
Critical for nerve function and energy. Vegetarians are at high risk of deficiency — consider a supplement if you eat no meat/eggs.
🥗 Best Indian Food Sources
EggsChickenFish (rohu, sardines, mackerel) Curd / DahiPaneerMilk Fortified idli/dosa batter (some brands)
💡 Best absorbed from animal sources. If vegetarian, eat curd + paneer daily and consider a B12 supplement.
🦴
Vitamin D
Bones · Calcium absorption · Mood
▼
Most Indians are deficient despite living in a sunny country — because we avoid direct sun. It controls calcium absorption and affects mood and immunity.
🥗 Best Indian Food Sources
Egg yolkFatty fish (mackerel, sardines, salmon) Fortified milkFortified curd Mushrooms (sun-exposed)
☀️ Best source: Sunlight. 15–20 mins of direct sun on arms/legs before 9am or after 4pm, 3–4 times a week is ideal.
💡 Vitamin D + Calcium work together. Always pair with calcium-rich foods for best effect.
🍊
Vitamin C
Immunity · Skin · Iron absorption
▼
Powerful antioxidant. Boosts immunity, helps iron absorb better, and keeps skin collagen intact. Cannot be stored — eat daily.
🥗 Best Indian Food Sources
Amla (Indian gooseberry) — highest source GuavaOrangeLemon / Nimbu Capsicum (especially red/yellow)Coriander leaves Curry leavesTomato PapayaPineapple
💡 Add lemon juice to dal or rice — it also helps absorb the iron from lentils better.
🧠
Vitamin B Complex (B1, B2, B3, B6, B9)
Energy · Nerves · Brain · Metabolism
▼
B vitamins convert food into energy. B9 (folate) is critical for cell repair. B6 supports brain health. Refined rice/maida strips these away — whole grains retain them.
🥗 Best Indian Food Sources
Brown rice / Hand-pounded riceMillets (ragi, jowar, bajra) SpinachMethi (fenugreek leaves)Drumstick leaves EggsChicken liver Dal (all types — toor, moong, masoor)Chana PeanutsSunflower seeds
💡 Switching even one meal from white rice to millets gives you a significant B vitamin boost.
🩸
Iron
Haemoglobin · Energy · Fatigue prevention
▼
Iron deficiency (anaemia) is extremely common in India, causing fatigue, breathlessness, and poor concentration. Vegetarian iron (non-heme) needs Vitamin C to absorb well.
🥗 Best Indian Food Sources
Chicken liverEggsFish SpinachMethiDrumstick leaves (moringa) Toor dalMasoor dalRajmaHorse gram (kulthi) Jaggery (paaghu/bella)Sesame seeds (til)Ragi
💡 Always squeeze lemon on dal or eat Vitamin C food in the same meal to double iron absorption.
🦴
Calcium
Bones · Teeth · Muscle function
▼
Essential for strong bones and teeth. Also needed for muscle contractions and nerve signals. Needs Vitamin D to absorb properly.
🥗 Best Indian Food Sources
Milk (200ml = ~240mg)Curd / DahiPaneerButtermilk Ragi (finger millet) — best plant sourceDrumstick leavesAmaranth Sesame seeds (til)Almonds RajmaChana dal
💡 Ragi mudde or ragi roti is one of the best calcium sources in Indian cuisine — better than most vegetables.
💪
Magnesium
Muscle recovery · Sleep · Energy
▼
Important for gym-goers — helps muscles relax and recover after workouts. Also improves sleep quality and reduces cramps.
🥗 Best Indian Food Sources
SpinachBanana Pumpkin seedsSunflower seeds AlmondsCashewsPeanuts Dark chocolate (70%+, small piece) Moong dalToor dal Whole wheat / Multigrain roti
💡 Gym workout depletes magnesium. A banana post-workout helps replenish it along with potassium.
🧬
Zinc
Immunity · Wound healing · Testosterone
▼
Zinc supports immune function, wound healing, and testosterone production — especially important for men who exercise. Deficiency causes hair loss and slow recovery.
🥗 Best Indian Food Sources
EggsChickenFish Pumpkin seedsCashewsAlmonds Chana (chickpea)RajmaMoong dal Sesame seedsWhole wheat
💡 Soaking and sprouting dals before cooking increases zinc bioavailability significantly.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids
Heart · Brain · Inflammation
▼
Reduces inflammation, supports brain function and heart health. Most Indians are deficient due to low fish consumption. Reduces fat storage tendency.
🥗 Best Indian Food Sources
Mackerel (bangda)SardinesRohu fishHilsa WalnutsFlaxseeds (alsi)Chia seeds Mustard oilMethi seeds
💡 Grind flaxseeds and add to your multigrain drink — invisible but very effective source of Omega-3.
🦠 Probiotics are live beneficial bacteria that live in your gut. A healthy gut microbiome improves digestion, immunity, mood, and even helps with weight loss by reducing inflammation and improving nutrient absorption. Include at least one probiotic food every day.
🥛
Curd / Dahi
Most accessible Indian probiotic
▼
Homemade curd is one of the best probiotics available in India. Contains Lactobacillus bacteria that line the gut and aid digestion. Always prefer homemade over store-bought sweet curd.
60–100g with lunchHomemade preferredFull-fat or low-fat both fine
💡 Eat at room temperature, not ice-cold. Cold curd kills some of the live bacteria.
🥤
Buttermilk / Chaas / Majjige
Cooling + gut-friendly
▼
Diluted curd with water and spices (jeera, curry leaves, ginger). Extremely good for digestion and only ~40 kcal per glass. Perfect post-lunch drink especially in summer.
1 glass after lunchAdd jeera + curry leavesNo added sugar
💡 Better than regular water post-meal — settles digestion and provides gut bacteria.
🫙
Idli / Dosa Batter (fermented)
Natural fermentation probiotic
▼
Properly fermented idli-dosa batter (12–16 hours fermentation) is rich in beneficial bacteria. The fermentation also increases B vitamins and reduces phytic acid, improving mineral absorption.
Idli (steamed — best)Dosa12–16 hr fermentation ideal
💡 Steamed idli retains more probiotics than pan-fried dosa. Have idli over dosa when possible.
🫚
Kanji (fermented rice water)
Traditional South Indian gut tonic
▼
Left-over cooked rice soaked overnight in water and eaten next morning is a traditional South Indian probiotic. Called "Pazhaya Sadam" in Tamil or "Pazham kanji" — this fermented rice is extremely gut-friendly.
Overnight soaked riceAdd a little curd or buttermilkMorning meal option
💡 A grandma's secret — this is being rediscovered by nutritionists as one of the best gut foods.
🥬
Pickles (Homemade / Naturally fermented)
Fermented vegetables
▼
Traditionally made Indian pickles (avakaya, maavadu, lime pickle) that are naturally fermented — not the vinegar-preserved commercial ones — contain beneficial bacteria. Small amounts with meals.
Homemade mango pickleLime pickle1 tsp with mealAvoid commercial (vinegar-based)
💡 Only naturally fermented (oil + salt method) pickles have probiotic value. Vinegar pickles do not.
🌾
Ambali / Koozh (fermented millet porridge)
Traditional gut + energy food
▼
Fermented ragi or kambu (bajra) porridge. Traditional breakfast in Tamil Nadu and Karnataka. The overnight fermentation multiplies beneficial bacteria and pre-digests the millet, making it very easy on the gut.
Ragi koozhKambu koozhAdd small onion + green chilli
💡 One of the most nutritious traditional breakfasts — provides calcium (ragi), probiotics, and slow-release energy.
🫘
Sprouted Legumes
Prebiotic — feeds your gut bacteria
▼
Sprouted moong, chana, and methi seeds are prebiotic — they feed the existing good bacteria in your gut rather than adding new ones. Think of prebiotics as food for probiotics.
Sprouted moongSprouted chanaSprouted methi seeds (small amount)BananaOnionGarlic
💡 Eat sprouted moong as a salad with lemon and chilli — raw or lightly steamed for maximum prebiotic benefit.
01
Eat 5 times a day — not 3
Research supports eating 5 smaller meals spaced every 3–4 hours rather than 3 large meals. This keeps blood sugar stable, reduces hunger spikes, and prevents overeating. Ideal schedule: 7am · 10:30am · 1pm · 4pm · 7:30pm. Your current meal plan already follows this — stick to it.
02
Eat slowly — take 20 minutes per meal
Your brain takes 20 minutes to register that your stomach is full. Eating fast leads to overeating before the signal arrives. Put down your spoon between bites, chew each mouthful 15–20 times. People who eat slowly naturally consume 10–15% fewer calories.
03
Eat protein first at every meal
Starting with dal, egg, curd, or chicken before touching the rice slows glucose absorption, keeps you full longer, and preserves muscle during fat loss. Aim for protein in every single meal. When you eat rice first, blood sugar spikes fast — then crashes — making you hungry again sooner.
04
Drink 500ml water before meals
Drinking 2 glasses of water 20–30 minutes before a meal reduces the amount you eat by an average of 13%. The water takes up stomach space and dulls hunger signals. Studies show this alone can create a 44% greater weight loss over 12 weeks compared to not doing it.
05
Stop eating 2–3 hours before bed
Your metabolism slows significantly at night. Food eaten after 9pm is more likely to be stored as fat because insulin sensitivity drops in the evening. Your dinner at 7:30pm is ideal — avoid eating after that except for plain water or herbal tea.
06
Use smaller plates — the visual trick that works
Serving food on a smaller plate (25cm instead of 30cm) reduces portions by 20–30% without feeling deprived — because your brain judges fullness by how full the plate looks, not by absolute quantity. This is one of the most consistently proven behavioural tricks in nutrition research.
07
Eat vegetables and salad first
Eat raw salad or cooked vegetables at the start of your meal before rice. Fibre from vegetables slows down carbohydrate digestion, flattens the blood sugar spike from rice, and fills up stomach volume with very few calories. Even a small kachumber salad (cucumber + tomato + onion + lemon) before rice makes a difference.
08
Sleep 7–8 hours — it's not optional for fat loss
Sleep deprivation increases ghrelin (hunger hormone) by 24% and decreases leptin (fullness hormone). This makes you crave high-calorie foods the next day and makes your body hold onto fat more stubbornly. Studies show people who sleep less than 6 hours lose 55% less fat even on the same diet.
09
Never skip post-gym nutrition
Within 45 minutes after your workout, your muscles are primed to absorb protein and carbohydrates for recovery. Skipping this window leads to muscle breakdown instead of muscle building. Your current post-gym meal (multigrain drink + banana + almonds) is well-timed — keep it consistent.
10
Reduce liquid calories — they don't fill you up
Tea with jaggery, juice, flavoured buttermilk, and lassi all carry calories but don't satisfy hunger like solid food does. Two cups of sweetened tea per day = ~120 kcal hidden. Replace one with black coffee or plain tea to quietly save 60 kcal per day = 1.8 kg saved per year.
11
Track everything — even small bites count
Studies consistently show people who track food lose twice as much weight as those who don't — even if both groups eat the same food. "Taste testing" while cooking, the last few bites of someone else's plate, and untracked snacks add up to 200–400 hidden calories per day. Log everything in this app — no exceptions.
12
Walk after meals — even 10 minutes helps
A brisk 10-minute walk within 30 minutes of a meal reduces the post-meal blood sugar spike by up to 30%. This means less fat storage from that meal. Even walking slowly around the house counts. Three 10-minute walks a day (after each main meal) adds up to 30+ extra minutes of calorie burning.
